# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is a technically fluent seminar presenter studying Jumper et al. (2021) with physics, ML, and scientific-computing instincts. Secondary users are students, instructors, and open-source contributors who want a paper-grounded way to inspect AlphaFold2's geometric ideas without first installing a full model stack.

## Product Purpose

AlphaFold 3D Companion is a local scientific workstation for understanding the original AlphaFold2 paper through linked simulations, curated examples, and optional local inference. Success means the user can move from a paper concept to an interactive geometry scene, then to a cached or locally generated structure, and explain what the visual result does and does not prove.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, instrument-like. The interface should feel like a research tool that happens to teach clearly, not like a marketing page or a decorative slide deck.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic dark SaaS dashboards, oversized landing-page heroes, decorative glassmorphism, animated spectacle that does not encode state, and anything that implies the deterministic simulator is a scientifically valid AlphaFold prediction.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve scientific honesty: every output states whether it is a toy simulation, cached artifact, fallback model, or true AF2-family run.
2. Link concepts across representations: selections should travel between contact maps, plots, frame diagrams, and structures.
3. Teach through manipulable invariants: sliders and toggles should reveal coevolution, triangle constraints, SE(3) invariance, FAPE, and recycling.
4. Keep the workstation dense but legible: controls, legends, and interpretation stay close to the visual state they explain.
5. Make open-source extension obvious: examples, scene specs, and adapters should be small, documented, and replaceable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast. Provide keyboard-accessible tabs and controls, visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives, semantic status text for errors/loading, and confidence encodings that combine color with labels and numeric values.
